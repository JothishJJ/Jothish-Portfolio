This blog is going to talk about one of my favorite things when it comes to life productivity, 
that's right in this blog we'll discuss how you can optimise your productivity when you're programming.
We'll explore essential strategies and mindsets to boost your focus, tackle complex tasks, and avoid burnout.

### Doing what you like
\
I am a big fan of doing what I like because well, it's something I like. When you are doing something that you
like you tend to put more effort into it, maybe it's something you're passionate about or something that gives you joy.
Whatever it is you should try to do it more.

The problem, with doing what you like is that, sometimes you can't decide if you get to work on something you like.
Maybe you are a big fan of Svelte, but your boss wants you to work on React instead. In those situations however, you
might need to find ways to make your job fun, because eventually you might end up burning out.

If you're working on building a portfolio, don't do projects for the sake of making a portfolio, but instead make something
you like, or something you need for yourself, instead of making the same todolist app in ten different frameworks, make something
you would use. If you use computers in general, you might find some problem for a solution may or may not exist, even if there is
a solution to your problem, just make it for the fun of it. You'll learn something non the less. Also be sure it's in your skill range.

Doing what you like, the productivity increase from it, I think is well written in a book called "Feel-Good Productivity" By Ali Abdaal.

### Do not try to Multitask
\
Multitasking may or may not seem like it will increase your productivity, but it won't. When doing a task your mind has to go in 
a flow state inorder to get peek productivity, where you can work for hours without interuptions. Now, when you are switching between
tasks, your mind can't get there cause you're constantly switching between your tasks.

When talking about multitasking I'm not just talking about multitasking as in doing other work, but all the tasks other than your
work. People somethimes say they like to work with music, personally I think that you can be more productive when you have it off, 
sometimes they'll also say that having music improves their productivity. Now, I don't know if having music, can really improve
your productivity or not so I wouldn't compell anyone, just decide on your own.

### Pomodoro Technique
\
This time management technique invloves doing your tasks for 25 minutes, and then taking a 5 minute break. Now, I'm sure that
in your programming journey you might have hours just trying to solve a problem, and just getting frustrated because you can't
find a solution, but after taking a quick break, you solve the problem almost immediately.

This is something I have done countless times. Sometimes when a problem arises, you have been trying to solve it for hours,
your brain tells you, being the best programmer in the world "I can solve this". Taking a quick break can really help you
refresh your brain.

I really reccommend this, you'll be way more productive by taking a break, and maybe go outside for a walk, catch some sun rays
and then go back to solving your problem.

### General Stuff
\
Programming involves staring at the computer screen for hours and sitting at the same place for a long time. You should
consider working out, walking in general would go a long way, though you should not limit it just there. If you can try getting 
a standing standing desk which will help you work while standing. And ofcourse eat good. It work atleast help you with better
health.

Other things like optimising or getting good at using your tools. I use Neovim btw, so obviously I am a 10x dev. You might be
using some other obsolite tools like vscode, or IntelliJ. In any case get good at using them, when using vscode for example
instead of using your mouse to go through the tree to get file you are looking for, use the fuzzy finder by pressing Ctrl + P.
No matter the tool your using, you can get good at them to improve your speed and productivity.

Another I would say underrated thing you should get good at is typing, ofcouse programming invloves a lot of typing, and maybe
you are saying to yourself that, I will get better eventually. That is true in some sence, but I really think you can just put
whatever you're thinking into your program as you're thinking. Just learning the basic finger position can really go a long way
I would also say to not stop there, but to improve your typing more.
