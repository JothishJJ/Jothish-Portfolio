In the world of AI everyone is obssesed with generative tools, and for good reason. It has a lot of hype around it, and even when we think the hype will die out it continues to go.

Every week, we're seeing breakthroughs in texts, images, content creation and building agents. I have noticed a space which could massively get into if we focus all in with the help of AI.

## Education

For the first time in history, we have the tools neccessory too deliver education to every single person on the planet.

In the current education system a few problems that might happen is

* Access to education - Millions of children doesn't have access to good schools or qualified teachers.
* No personilisation - Not everyone has a private teacher explaining the concepts deeply enough, for a everyone's learning style.
* Desire to Learn - Traditional education often kills curiosity. Learning feels forced instead of chosen.

<br/>

There are numerous studies showing the less number of students in a classroom, correlate to more effective grasp of the topics taught.
However, it is every expensive to scale this quality.
There's the lack of good teachers who needs to teach relavent topics and make sure that the students understand what's being taught, and mind you there needs to be a single teacher for everysingle student.
And currently it is also expensive for good quality education, you check out the Ivy Leagues in America, and more colleges and even schools which are not government based, will surely be much expensive.


## How will AI play a role?

AI, can teach the students very easily and according to their learning style.

* **Instant feedback —** no more waiting for assignments to be graded

* **Adaptive teaching —** explain the same concept in different ways until it clicks

* **Shame free learning —** students can ask “dumb” questions without fear

* **24/7 support —** available anytime, in any language

AI could explain quantum physics to a 10 year old or walk a curious adult through the basics of programming—at their own pace, in their own style.


## Problems with AI

AI, will replace teachers. Okay, maybe not entirely since it does have flaws. First of all, AI works by predicting which means that there is a chance that it might predict missinformation while replying. It will be in full confidence when it does that.
Second, there is also the problem of spoon feeding with AI, AI has to teach. A lot of people think that difficulty in learning is a problem, while it can be true for certain number of cases, it is also true that the more difficult you feel during learning, the harder you're learning.
We don't want AI to be another fancy version of Google.
Lastly, I think this is the biggest one of all, the desire to learn. I don't AI, isn't solving the problem of "Desire for learning", students can't be forced to learn, well they can be, but its much more effective, especially in the long run if they themselves come forward wanting to learn. It will promote them to go deep into topics they like, or are curious about, and AI isn't solving that. Atleast not yet.

However, I do think that people can solve these problems and if we do a great job at making it happen it could unlock the way to education for many. There is a great quote around this "The cure to cancer might be in the head of a underpriliged child"
